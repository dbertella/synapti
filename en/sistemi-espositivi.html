Exhibition System

        The workshop's purpose is to design and realise multi-sensorial 
        exhibition systems for art works and top-rank commercial products.
        The conceptual platform of such systems comes from 
        a neurobiological code, which avails itself 
        of two innovative tools:

        A )  The use of a new language defined "sub-symbolic", 
        which enables the art work to self-present the audience 
        the genesis of its own anatomic inner composition 
        and of the intrinsic meanings emerging from its physical, perceptive 
        and functional aspects.
        This way its spacial configuration turns into 
        a perfect narrative sequence. 
        A story is created, then.

        B )  The use of exhibition bubbles, which wrap the observer ( more precisely small groups of observers) 
        with a flow of multisensorial information, which get assembled according to the principle of 
        " a parallel and distributed representation " of neural networks 
        ( see Colecchia, "The Attractive Power of Art Works", Amazon).
        This expressive system is able to get into resonance with the observer's body 
        before expanding itself into his rational thought, 
        which is therefore unconsciously influenced by it . 
        But all this is neither  a subliminal effect, nor Freudian unconscious instinct, 
        rather it has something with energetic resonance studied by Physics 
        and with the connective patterns studied by Neuroscience.
        The usefulness of this discovery  may floor the operators of the exhibition sector.
